The problem

Enterprises stall on AI for one reason: loss of control.

A swarm of autonomous agents that can each spend money, call vendors, and top up APIs is a productivity dream and a governance nightmare. One hallucinated cost, one duplicate charge, one prompt-injected "urgent wire," and both the budget and the trust are gone. Closed frontier APIs make it worse: sensitive spend data leaves your infrastructure, and per-token costs across a 52-agent swarm are unpredictable. For regulated clients in finance, healthcare, and government, that is a non-starter.

The solution

A governed gate on every action, self-hosted by design.

Budget Sentinel runs an open-weight model on your own infrastructure. For each proposed action it returns a structured assessment: category, estimated cost, risk flags, confidence. That advisory is validated against a strict contract and handed to a deterministic policy engine that owns the verdict. Nothing the model says can bypass a budget cap or a policy rule. Anything uncertain, over-limit, or flagged is routed to a human, and every decision, machine or human, is chained into an audit ledger you can replay and prove.

MODEL

Advise

Local Qwen3 assesses the action. It never decides or acts.

CONTRACT

Validate

Output is schema-checked. Malformed input fails closed to a human.

RULES

Decide

Deterministic caps and policy own the verdict: auto, hold, block, escalate.

HUMAN

Approve the 20%

People clear the risky slice. Every call is logged.

Three benefits

Control that leadership can actually sign off on.

◈

Data sovereignty

The model runs on your hardware. Spend data, vendor names, and PO details never leave your infrastructure: the requirement DIFC-finance, healthcare, and government clients cannot waive.

▤

Budget control

Hard, versioned caps per category and per action. An open-weight model is cents-per-thousand-decisions, so the whole 52-agent swarm stays inside a predictable, provable budget line.

⛓

Tamper-evident audit

Every decision is hash-chained into an append-only ledger. Change one past entry and verification breaks. Replay the whole chain to prove exactly what was approved, by whom, under which policy.

Use case · the OpenClaw swarm

52 agents, one budget line, zero surprises.

In an OpenClaw deployment, dozens of agents run procurement, tooling, and API spend around the clock. Budget Sentinel sits in front of all of them: routine top-ups and small tooling costs clear automatically, over-cap GPU bursts and unknown-vendor invoices are held, fraud-pattern requests are blocked outright, and ambiguous items escalate, each one recorded. Leadership watches a single board and only touches the exceptions. That is the 80/20 model, deployed branch by branch, expanded only after the ledger proves it safe.
