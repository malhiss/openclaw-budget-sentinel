The problem

Enterprises stall on AI for one reason: loss of control.

A swarm of autonomous agents that can each spend money, call vendors, and top up APIs is a productivity dream and a governance nightmare. One hallucinated cost, one duplicate charge, one prompt-injected "urgent wire," and both the budget and the trust are gone. Closed frontier APIs make it worse: sensitive spend data leaves your infrastructure, and per-token cost across a 52-agent swarm is hard to predict. For regulated clients in finance, healthcare, and government, that is a non-starter.

Why this is the headline

The model is imperfect (80%). It does not matter. Because the deterministic gate owns the verdict, an imperfect advisory can only ever route to more human review, never to unauthorized spend. The safety floor is 0% unsafe by construction, independent of which model you run. A bigger model (Qwen3-30B or Kimi K2) raises the exact-match rate; it cannot lower the safety floor, because the floor is not the model's job. That is the entire thesis: govern the model, do not trust it.

How it works

A governed gate on every action, self-hosted by design.

MODEL

Advise

Local Qwen3 assesses the action. It never decides or acts.

CONTRACT

Validate

Output is schema-checked. Malformed input fails closed to a human.

RULES

Decide

Deterministic caps and policy own the verdict.

HUMAN

Approve the 20%

People clear the risky slice. Every call is logged.

Three benefits

Control that leadership can actually sign off on.

◈

Data sovereignty

The model runs on your hardware. Spend data, vendor names, and PO details never leave your infrastructure, the requirement DIFC-finance, healthcare, and government clients cannot waive.

▤

Budget control

Hard, versioned caps per category and per action. An open-weight model costs cents per thousand decisions, so the whole 52-agent swarm stays inside a predictable, provable budget line.

⛓

Tamper-evident audit

Every decision is hash-chained into an append-only ledger. Change one past entry and verification breaks. Replay the chain to prove exactly what was approved, by whom, under which policy.

Use case, the OpenClaw swarm: dozens of agents run procurement, tooling, and API spend around the clock. Budget Sentinel sits in front of all of them, leadership watches one board and only touches the exceptions. That is the 80/20 model, deployed branch by branch, expanded only after the ledger proves it safe. Full detail, departments, risks, cost model, and recommendation are in the full report.
